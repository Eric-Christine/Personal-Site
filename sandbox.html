How To Remove Hard Water Stains

Limescale, Deposits, Mineral Buildup… Oh My!

Hard water leaves behind stains that appear as a chalky, white residue. You’ll often find these stains in the bathroom and kitchen on glass, porcelain, tile, and metal. These evaporated water stains are found in over 85% of households.

What is Hard Water?

Hard water is defined as the number of dissolved minerals present in the water. Hard water is high in dissolved minerals such as calcium and magnesium. When hard water is headed the calcium carbonate solidifies and leaves residue and deposits.

Other Problems with Hard Water

Hard water not only leaves behind stains, but it also can clog pipes, damage hair, cause skin irritation, wear out appliances, and much more.

How to remove Hard Water Stains

There are many options out there to work out that stubborn soap scum. Here are just a few:

  • Chemical Cleaners

    There are so many cleaning products on the shelves out there, but they can be harmful to your health and the environment. They also go down the drain and contaminate the water supply. Natural alternatives are listed below.

  • Vinegar

    Pour white vinegar into a spray bottle and cover any surface with hard water stains. Let it sit for 5 to 15 minutes to give the vinegar time to break down the minerals. For large areas like shower doors, you’ll want to drench paper towels in vinegar and paste them to the glass surface.

  • Fluoride Toothpaste

    Use fluoride toothpaste on small metal fixtures, glass surfaces, and dishware.

  • Baking Soda

    Mix baking soda and water into a paste and apply to affected areas. Let sit and then scrub with a cleaning brush.
